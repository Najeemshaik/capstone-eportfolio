In this assignment, you will create your ePortfolio, starting with an About Me page a Project Entry, and Reflection following the guidelines provided below. 

 

About Me Page: 
The About Me page should be separate from your reflection and project entry page. It must include a brief introduction to who you are, your background, and your professional interests. Be sure to provide context on your academic journey, skills, and ambitions, giving the reader a sense of who you are as an individual. 

 What to Include on Your "About Me" Page: 

A concise professional bio: your academic background, relevant skills, and career aspirations. 
Optional but encouraged, a headshot or professional photo  
Links to other projects or achievements that showcase your work. 
A brief description of your personal interests, as appropriate, that might help the reader understand your motivations and character. 
Contact information or a call-to-action if you want readers to engage with you beyond the ePortfolio. 
 

Project Entry: 
Create an ePortfolio entry about your Capstone project as if you are presenting it to a professional engineer from the industry, using the following guidelines: 

Write a solid, yet short, story narrative that discusses what you did (outcomes), why you did it (objectives), and how you achieved it (processes and contributions). 
Your narrative of learning process should reflect the progression or iteration of your design. Don’t simply describe the final outcome, illustrate how you arrived at it, showcasing the evolution of your solution, from concept to final prototype.  
Ensure your ePortfolio entry addresses both Non-Engineering and Engineering perspectives: provide a clear description for those without a technical background and include images or graphs for engineers who may prefer visual representations with minimal explanation. 
Your ePortfolio entry should be clear and concise, presenting only the most essential information to tell your project's story. Avoid long paragraphs or too many details that might overwhelm the reader. The focus should be on the key elements that convey the essence of your project, making it easy to follow.  
Your multimedia is your biggest advantage, use it to guide the reader through your prototype process and iterative designs from beginning to end, incorporating low-fidelity sketches or simulations as necessary. You must use descriptive captions to provide additional context and help the viewer's understanding. You can choose your multimedia from options such as interactable components, GitHub links, images, videos, animations, or GIFs.  
Present content in a creative and visually engaging manner. Ensure that the design is consistently cohesive, with a unified color scheme, typography, and layout that effectively enhance the content. The reader should be able to view all the key information about your project in one website page. No navigation should be required to access your project’s full content. 
 

Reflection:
Create a separate page dedicated to reflecting in 300 words, structured in the first person, on your learning and growth by addressing the following questions: 

What did you learn about the design process from your experience in this course? 
Based on your experiences in this course, what are the most important attributes of an engineer? 
How has your experience on this project (or in other courses) contributed to your understanding of the role and responsibilities of the professional engineer in society? 
Based on what you learned about yourself in this course and over your time in the School of Engineering, what strengths do you bring to your engineering career? Which areas need growth or development, and how will you facilitate that growth? 
 

Remember, the About Me, Project Entry, and Reflection pages must be separate. The ePortfolio entry should be as website URL.
